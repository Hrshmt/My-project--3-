Key Terms of Treaty 1

  • Land Surrender: Land Surrender The First Nations agreed to cede approximately 160,000 square kilometers of their traditional lands to the Crown for European settlement and agricultural development. this page.
  • Reserve Creation: Small parts of land, known as reserves, were set aside for each First Nation, calculated at 160 acres per family of five. These reserves were meant to be the permanent homes for the First Nations. this page.
  • Financial Compensation: Each First Nation member was to receive: A one-time payment of $3 per person upon signing the treaty. An annual annuity of $3 per person and $15 for each chief. Chiefs and headmen were given suits of clothing as part of the agreement. this page.
  • Right to Hunt and Fish: First Nations retained the right to continue hunting and fishing on ceded lands, except on lands required for settlement, mining, or other development. this page.
  • Education: The treaty included provisions for establishing schools on reserves to promote education among First Nations children.

Impact

The signing of Treaty 1 in 1871 at Lower Fort Garry, established the framework for the relationship between the Canadian government and the First Nations in southern Manitoba.
