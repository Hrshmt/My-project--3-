What is Treaty 1?

treaty 1, also known as the stone fort treaty, is a foundational agreement between the canadian government and several first nations in what is now southern manitoba. it was signed on august 3, 1871, at lower fort garry near present-day selkirk, manitoba.

lieutenant governor adams george archibald was the first lieutenant governor of manitoba and the northwest territories, tasked with overseeing the development of the newly created province. he represented the crown in the treaty process and played a key role in ensuring that the terms of the treaty aligned with the government's goals of settlement and agricultural expansion. this page.

Staff and military presence: the crown's representatives were accompanied by a small team of legal advisors, clerks, and interpreters to assist with drafting and translating the treaty. for more information about the legal advisors, visit this page.
